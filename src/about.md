## A little introduction...

My name is Laura and I'm a 25 year old Colombian programmer with a passion for video games, software, art and all things creative!

Coding and art have been huge parts of my life since I was a tiny little human. My mom being a fashion designer and my dad being a programmer meant I had both in my blood.

Currently I'm focused on web development (it IS my job after all) and my side projects mostly consist of game development or game art related stuff, like Minecraft mods and resource packs, video game pixel art assets and of course, actual video games. Oh, and this website... duh.

If you want to dive a little deeper into what I laki and why and my journey through art and code, prepare to read. I've tried to summarize it, but I can't not make this story long hehe...

### Artistric background

I am by no means a super successfull or well-known artist. In fact, it's been a looong while since I last posted some art online (until I made this website of course). At first I just used to draw for myself, friends and family, but when I discovered a certain little show about pastel colored horses, I was inspired to make fanart and share it online.

### Coding and games

Around 2013 I had my very first taste of programming when I installed a Minecraft mod called "ComputerCraft", which, as the name implies, adds computers to Minecraft! Turns out these computers actually allowed you to create simple text-based programs with a programming language based on Lua, and it blew my mind! I never made anything super cool with it, I was just a little kid who strugled to understand what the hell a function even is, but the bug had bit me.

Since then I had various periods where I tried to learn programming and be good enough to make cool stuff; I learned HTML and CSS, I tried to learn to make games in Unity, completely failed to understand the difference between a class and an object and what it mean to return a value, try out some visual programming with Scratch, learned HTML and CSS again because I forgot everything, learned python, tried to learn to make Roblox "experiences" (or whatever they call it these days), learned HTML and CSS yet AGAIN and for the last time (although you never stop learning), actually learned Javascript and here, it clicks. I don't know what clicks, but it clicks. I now understand classes and objects, I even start to understand inheritance, return values, arguments vs parameters. I start finding it easier to solve problems by myself. I start thinking like a programmer.

Fast forward to 2019, I have more experience with software development, but meanwhile, Windows is starting to frustrate me. The lack of customization (that somehow gets worse and worse with each version because who needs individuality and expression in this day and age?), the poor performance on my (admitedly) kinda crappy laptop at the time, the programs I know I don't need or want but I cannot uninstall because Microsoft says "Nope! lol" so I can't free up that space, and the god damn ADS. It's driving me crazy. So naturally, I look for alternatives and of course, you know where this end up leading.

I install my first Linux distribution, Linux Mint and it felt like HEAVEN. It runs so fast, there's no ads where there shouldn't be, I can make my system look exactly how I want, if I don't like something I can literally just change it (literally ANYTHING). Of course this comes with its own problems that I won't get into (maybe I'll post about this on some other occasion), but it is SO worth it. There's almost nothing that can't be solved, and this naturally leads me to learn even MORE about programming, and software in general. I start getting comfortable in the terminal to the point where I'm constantly searching for TUI (Terminal User Interface) alternatives to popular programs and tools for different aspects of my life. Postman? Nah, I'm happy with <a href="#">Posting</a>, thank you very much. VSCode? Nope! It's cool, but nothing beats my custom Neovim configuration. If you can think of it, there's a TUI for it somewhere, and it's beautiful.

So yeah. I love terminal interfaces, I love the vibe of it. There's just something I find relaxing and comfortable about a minimalistic terminal-style UI, and I even like games that use such a style (check out Caves of Quud). This is why my website looks like this. It make me feel right at home.
